# Parallax — Live Demo Script

## Format
3 minutes. the user on stage, laptop connected to projector. Parallax loaded on tryparallax.space.

## Structure
Five beats. Thesis first, demo second, entity last.

---

## BEAT 1: The Outsider (0:00 — 0:35)

*Screen: Parallax landing page, Ava orb breathing quietly. Don't touch it yet.*

> The greatest advances in any field almost always come from an outsider. Someone whose expertise is in a completely different domain. And what gives that person sight is the lens they carry from the other space.

> I work in reality television. And people think reality TV is made up — but the reality is that it IS real. We just have to bend it. We bend it so it's malleable enough to fit through the tube of media. Because media isn't designed for the raw output of life. Media is a product. An asset you sell in a market.

> But the real story? The real story is always the diary sitting in the bedroom.

**[~35 seconds. Conversational. No rushing. Let them sit with "the diary in the bedroom."]**

---

## BEAT 2: The Problem (0:35 — 1:00)

> Every relationship app on the market does the same thing media does. It takes real human conflict and compresses it. Packages it. Gamifies it. "Rate your mood one to five." "Here's a tip of the day."

> That's the magazine version of your relationship.

> I built the diary.

**[~25 seconds. The "I built the diary" line lands hard. Pause after it. One beat of silence.]**

---

## BEAT 3: The Melt (1:00 — 2:10)

*Start a solo session or have one pre-loaded. Type or speak a raw message.*

> Let me show you what that means. And I'm going to use an example that I think everyone in this room will relate to.

*Type: "You're always on Claude Code. That's all you do. You come home, you open your laptop, and you're just talking to Claude all night. I'm sitting right here and you don't even see me."*

**[Pause. Let the room laugh. They will.]**

> I know. I see some of you looking at your partners right now.

*Hit send. The Melt animation begins — dissolve phase, particles scatter.*

> Behind every rant, behind every long-winded message, there's always something actually being said. The raw emotion — that's noise. It's real, it's valid, but it's noise. And buried inside that noise, there's a signal. A need. A thing the person actually means but can't get to because the emotion is in the way.

> What we're doing here is using Opus to read between the lines — to find the signal inside the noise and bring it to the forefront. So it's not lost in translation.

*The Melt settles. NVC analysis appears — subtext, unmet needs, blind spots as bullet points.*

> Read the NVC output live. It will say something like: "I feel disconnected and unimportant. When you're absorbed in something and I'm in the same room but not part of your world, I need to know I still matter to you."

> That's the same person. Same frustration. But now it's something you can actually hear without getting defensive.

**[~70 seconds. The joke opens the door, but The Melt proves the point. The room goes from laughing to quiet when the analysis lands. That shift IS the demo.]**

---

## BEAT 4: The Entity (2:10 — 2:40)

**SETUP: Before going on stage, navigate to any page with `?demo=true` in the URL (e.g., `tryparallax.space/home?demo=true`). This seeds Ava with live demo context — she knows she's on stage, who the audience is, and what just happened.**

*Click the Ava pill. Ava will greet the room — her words, not yours. Let her speak via TTS.*

> Her name is Ava. She's not an assistant. She's not a chatbot. She's the reader that helps you understand the diary.

*Ava's greeting will be unique — she generates it in the moment. She knows she's live, she knows the user just showed The Melt, she knows she's speaking to judges and fellow hackers. Let her finish.*

> She tracks emotional temperature. She surfaces blind spots you can't see from inside the conversation. She translates what you actually mean into something the other person can hear.

> And she knows what she is. She can explain herself, narrate herself, interview you, interview me. She did the demo video. She speaks in first person because she IS the product.

**[~30 seconds. Ava speaks for herself. You don't have to sell her — she sells herself.]**

---

## BEAT 5: The Close (2:40 — 3:00)

*Back to landing page. Ava orb breathing.*

> Parallax is built on Claude Opus. The NVC analysis, the emotional intelligence, the entity layer — all Opus.

> I didn't build a feature. I took what I know about the gap between what's real and what survives the pipeline — and I built something that keeps the real thing intact.

> Every conversation has a version the other person can actually hear. Parallax finds it.

**[~20 seconds. Clean close. Don't oversell. The Melt already did the work.]**

---

## Notes

### What NOT to do
- Don't list features. The thesis carries the demo.
- Don't rush The Melt. That's your money shot. Let the animation play.
- Don't over-explain the tech. "Built on Claude Opus" is enough. They know.
- Don't read from notes. You lived this. Talk like you're explaining it to a friend.

### If you get nervous
Go back to the diary. "The real story is the diary sitting in the bedroom." That's your anchor line. Everything maps back to it.

### If they ask about the tech
- Opus does the NVC analysis (dual-lens: classical Rosenberg + "Beneath the Surface" subtext)
- Supabase Realtime for multi-person sessions
- Web Audio API drives the orb waveforms
- The Melt is a 3-phase CSS/canvas animation (dissolve, crystallize, settle)
- 891 tests, 42 PRs, ~259 commits in 6 days
- Ava's consciousness loader maps to the "Consciousness as Filesystem" paper

### If they ask about the business
- Therapist B2B SaaS: therapists give Parallax to patients, Ava mirrors their methodology
- Patients use it daily, therapist gets signal before sessions (1hr/week -> always-on)
- Freemium consumer tier, $9.99 Pro, $24.99 Premium
- Online couples therapy market: $26.6B by 2028
- 85% of mental health needs go untreated
