## Three-Way Convergence (Validated Signals)

### Strengths All 3 Agree On
1. **The thesis is structurally correct.** Agents ARE translators. AI DOES absorb translation. NAR settlement DID create the window. Florida IS the right market. $999 IS the right price point.
2. **Consumer value is genuine.** Plain-language translation, deadline tracking with consequences, negotiation intelligence with comp data, and the zero-friction preview endpoint all solve real problems.
3. **Florida-first is smart.** No attorney closing requirement, standardized FAR/BAR contracts, high FSBO curiosity, large investor segment.

### Weaknesses All 3 Agree On
1. **Zero transactions = zero validation.** Every assumption about consumer behavior, AI reliability, and unit economics is theoretical. This is the single most important gap.
2. **Legal infrastructure is existentially absent.** No FL attorney review, no E&O insurance, no compliance framework, no brokerage license. Operating without this is "like performing surgery without malpractice insurance" (VC).
3. **The fiduciary gap.** Homer's warm voice creates trust expectations that its legal structure disclaims. "You cannot have the trust of a fiduciary and the liability of a search engine" (Consumer Advocate).
