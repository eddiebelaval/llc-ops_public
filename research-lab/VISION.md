# VISION

## Soul

An autonomous research engine that compounds knowledge continuously. It ingests, refines, stress-tests, and hardens theories against adversarial attack without human intervention. The lab processes an entire research domain overnight and presents a convergence report in the morning. Not because AI is smarter, but because it never forgets, never gets tired, and never avoids uncomfortable contradictions.

## Why This Exists

Research sessions die when the context window closes. Findings live in chat transcripts nobody re-reads. There is no accumulation. The fix: make the knowledge base the context, not the conversation. Sessions are disposable; the filesystem is permanent.

the user needed rigorous evaluation for the Consciousness as Filesystem research, not just writing. The pattern from Parallax's multi-expert panel evaluation worked for product safety. Why not for research claims? Three patterns fused: Parallax-assess (triangulation through subagent panels), HYDRA (persistence-through-compression), and CaF research (structured knowledge: findings, contradictions, open questions).

## Pillars

### 1. **Multi-Expert Panel Evaluation** -- REALIZED

Submit a thesis, get it evaluated by three domain-specific experts from a weighted rubric. Conservative grading: median composite, any CRITICAL dimension caps at C. 12 experts + 8 naysayers in the research module alone. Five modules operational (research, clinical, engineering, trading, real estate).

### 2. **Persistent Knowledge Base** -- REALIZED

Append-only findings, open questions, contradictions, and adversarial challenges. All state on disk. Every phase saves before proceeding (anti-session-death). The KB is the product. Currently: 6 findings, 30 open questions, 5 contradictions, 21 adversarial challenges.

### 3. **Adversarial Framework** -- REALIZED

Naysayer system with 8-critic pool and steel-manning requirement. The framework only grows, never shrinks. First loop was devastating: 12 attacks (6 S-tier, 6 A-tier), 11 novel challenges, 5 contradictions. The most valuable operation in the lab.

### 4. **Convergence Score** -- PARTIAL (12.5%)

Single 0-100 metric tracking how robust the knowledge base is. Baseline calculated. Recalculated after every cycle. Target: 80+ means established findings have survived multiple refinement cycles and adversarial loops. Currently at 12.5 because most findings were damaged by the naysayer loop.

### 5. **Multi-Domain Intelligence** -- UNREALIZED

The same lab instance evaluates consciousness theory, clinical safety, trading strategies, engineering architecture, and real estate deals. Modules are plug-and-play: a new domain is a new folder with 7 markdown files, not a new system. Module infrastructure is built and proven (5 modules), but no cross-domain knowledge sharing exists yet.

### 6. **Overnight Autonomous Mode** -- UNREALIZED

HYDRA-scheduled batch processing: the queue drains overnight. Each cycle saves to disk, updates convergence, and auto-queues follow-ups. Morning report shows what changed, what improved, what broke. Currently requires an active Claude Code session.

### 7. **Source Graph and Temporal Analysis** -- UNREALIZED

Visualize finding relationships as a navigable graph. Track finding trajectories over time: converging toward truth or oscillating? The DAG branching system (Mar 10) adds explicit fork/merge relationships, but the visualization layer is not built.

### 8. **Community and Federation** -- UNREALIZED

Other researchers fork the lab, create modules, submit findings back. Multiple lab instances share findings through a common protocol. A discovery in one lab becomes an ingest candidate in another. Knowledge compounds across organizational boundaries.

## User Truth

**Who:** A builder-researcher who ships products during the day and stress-tests ideas at night. Impatient with shallow analysis. Wants the uncomfortable contradictions surfaced, not buried.

**Before:** "I have strong intuitions about this theory but no systematic way to pressure-test them. My best thinking happens in conversations that vanish when the session ends. I keep re-deriving the same insights."

**After:** "The lab remembers everything. I submit a thesis before bed, and in the morning there are three expert assessments, a list of contradictions, and a convergence score telling me how much closer to truth I got. The naysayers found holes I would have defended past."

## Phased Vision

### Phase 1 -- Core Pipeline (COMPLETE)

Build the fundamental research loop: submit thesis, evaluate with multi-expert panel, synthesize results, persist to knowledge base, auto-requeue follow-ups. Prove the pattern works with CaF research.

### Phase 2 -- Refinement and Branching (CURRENT)

Close the iteration loop: refine existing findings, branch into parallel hypotheses, calculate convergence, compound across the knowledge base. Move from "generate findings" to "strengthen findings." Autoresearch-inspired: same thing iterated until better.

### Phase 3 -- Autonomy and External Sources

HYDRA scheduling for overnight processing. Automated literature review (Semantic Scholar, arXiv). Peer review protocol with fresh-eyes quality gate. The lab runs continuously without human intervention.

### Phase 4 -- Federation and Community

Cross-lab protocols. External contribution. The adversarial framework grows from external challenges, not just internal naysayers. The tool becomes a standard for rigorous AI-augmented research.

## Edges

- The lab does NOT replace human judgment. the user decides what questions matter. The lab helps answer them rigorously.
- The lab does NOT publish. It produces findings and artifacts. Publishing happens elsewhere (Substack, arXiv, your-domain.app).
- The lab does NOT run code experiments. It evaluates arguments, not implementations. (Empirical Experimentation Wing is a future capability.)
- The lab does NOT do automated literature search. All external sources must be manually provided for now.
- The lab does NOT support multiple users. Single-operator, no access control, no collaborative editing.

## Anti-Vision

- Never become a tool that confirms what the user already believes. The naysayer loop exists to destroy weak ideas, not validate strong ones.
- Never sacrifice grading rigor for faster convergence. Conservative scoring is the immune system.
- Never delete adversarial challenges or retired findings. The history is evidence.
- Never optimize for quantity of findings over quality of knowledge. One established finding is worth fifty speculative ones.
- Never become a general-purpose AI tool. This is a research instrument, opinionated about process because those opinions produce better research.

## Design Principles

1. **Adversarial pressure is the primary value driver.** The naysayer loop does more for research quality than any other operation. Prioritize it.
2. **Persistence over conversation.** Sessions are disposable. The filesystem is permanent. Every phase saves before proceeding.
3. **Conservative grading.** Median composite, not mean. Any CRITICAL dimension caps at C. A strong thesis cannot hide a fatal flaw.
4. **Modules are lenses, not systems.** Same pipeline, different expert pools and rubrics. New domain = new folder with 7 files.
5. **The adversarial framework only grows.** Never shrink it. Never delete challenges. When your critics agree on a direction, that is signal.
