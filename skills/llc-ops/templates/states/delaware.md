# State Compliance: Delaware

## Key Advantages
- Most business-friendly legal framework in the US
- Court of Chancery (specialized business court, no juries)
- Strong LLC member protections
- Privacy: member names not in public filings

## Annual Requirements
| Filing | Due Date | Cost | Where |
|--------|----------|------|-------|
| Annual Report | Jun 1 | $300 | Delaware Division of Corporations |
| Annual Tax | Jun 1 | $300 (flat fee for LLCs) | corp.delaware.gov |

## Critical Dates
| Date | Event | Consequence if Missed |
|------|-------|----------------------|
| Mar 1 | Annual report due (corporations, not LLCs) | N/A for LLCs |
| Jun 1 | LLC Annual Tax due | $200 late penalty + 1.5%/month interest |
| Ongoing | Good standing requires tax current | Voided if delinquent |

## Formation Details
- Filing: Certificate of Formation via corp.delaware.gov
- Cost: $90
- Registered Agent: Required, must be DE address (services ~$50-300/yr)
- Operating Agreement: Highly recommended, DE law defers to it extensively
- If you live outside DE: you likely also need to register as a foreign LLC in your home state

## Important Notes
- **DE LLC + Live Elsewhere = Two Filings:** You must register in your home state too
- Total annual cost: $300 (DE) + home state registration + registered agent fee
- This makes DE expensive for small businesses unless you specifically need DE legal protections
- Best for: companies expecting litigation, multiple members, or venture funding

## Tax Considerations
- No state income tax on LLCs that don't operate within Delaware
- If you DO operate in DE: 8.7% corporate income tax rate
- No sales tax in Delaware
