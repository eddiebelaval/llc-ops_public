# State Compliance: Texas

## Key Advantages
- No state income tax (personal)
- Relatively low filing costs
- Business-friendly regulatory environment

## Annual Requirements
| Filing | Due Date | Cost | Where |
|--------|----------|------|-------|
| Franchise Tax Report | May 15 | Varies | Texas Comptroller |
| Public Information Report | May 15 | $0 (filed with franchise tax) | Texas Comptroller |

## Franchise Tax
- **No Tax Due Threshold:** $2,470,000 total revenue (2024)
- Below threshold: File "No Tax Due" report (still must file)
- Above threshold: 0.375% (retail/wholesale) or 0.75% (other) of taxable margin
- **EZ Computation:** 0.331% of total revenue (if revenue under $20M)

## Critical Dates
| Date | Event | Consequence if Missed |
|------|-------|----------------------|
| Jan 1 | Franchise tax period begins | N/A |
| May 15 | Franchise Tax + PIR due | Penalties + potential forfeiture |
| Late filing | After May 15 | 5% penalty (1-30 days), 10% (31+ days) |

## Formation Details
- Filing: Certificate of Formation via SOSDirect (sos.state.tx.us)
- Cost: $300
- Registered Agent: Required, must be TX address
- Operating Agreement: Called "Company Agreement" in TX, not filed

## Local Considerations
- No state income tax but property taxes are high (1.6-2.5% of assessed value)
- Some cities have additional business permits
- Sales tax: 6.25% state + up to 2% local
- Texas has broad sales tax on services (more than most states)
