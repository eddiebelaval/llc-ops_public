## Tax Strategy Priorities
1. **S-Corp Election** -- Critical for consultants earning $80k+. Pay reasonable salary, take rest as distribution to avoid SE tax on excess.
2. **SEP IRA / Solo 401(k)** -- Shelter up to $69k/year from taxes
3. **QBI Deduction** -- 20% deduction (income limits apply for "specified service trades")
4. **Home Office** -- Regular method often better for consultants
5. **Vehicle Deduction** -- Standard mileage (70c/mi in 2025) vs actual expenses
6. **Income Timing** -- Defer invoicing to manage tax brackets year-to-year

### S-Corp Decision Matrix
| Annual Net Profit | Recommendation |
|-------------------|----------------|
| Under $50k | Stay disregarded entity (SMLLC) |
| $50k-$80k | Analyze -- depends on your situation |
| $80k-$150k | S-Corp likely saves $5k-15k/year in SE tax |
| $150k+ | S-Corp strongly recommended |

### QBI Warning for Consultants
"Specified Service Trades or Businesses" (SSTB) -- which includes consulting -- have lower QBI income thresholds:
- Single: phases out $191,950-$241,950
- Married: phases out $383,900-$483,900
Above these thresholds, QBI deduction disappears for consultants.

## Insurance Recommendations
| Type | Priority | Estimated Cost | Why |
|------|----------|---------------|-----|
| Professional Liability (E&O) | REQUIRED | $500-2,000/yr | Covers errors, bad advice, negligence claims |
| General Liability | HIGH | $400-800/yr | Slip-and-fall at client site, property damage |
| Cyber Liability | MEDIUM-HIGH | $500-1,500/yr | If you handle any client data |

## Key Risk Areas
- **Scope Creep:** Clear contracts with defined deliverables and change order process
- **Accounts Receivable:** Net-30 terms can create cash flow gaps. Consider deposits.
- **Contractor Misclassification:** If you use regular subcontractors, ensure proper classification
- **Non-Compete / Non-Solicit:** Know your obligations from prior employment
- **Client Concentration:** If one client is >50% of revenue, you're exposed

## Tracking Recommendations
- Billable vs non-billable hours
- Revenue per client
- Average project value
- Pipeline value (proposals out)
- Invoice aging (days to payment)
- Effective hourly rate (total revenue / total hours)
